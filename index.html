Al-masjid an nabawiy

Al-Masjid an-Nabawi was the third mosque built in the history of Islam and is now one of the largest mosques in the world. It is the second-holiest site in Islam, after Masjid al-Haram in Mecca. The site was originally adjacent to Muhammad's house; he settled there after his Hijra (emigration) to Medina in 622 CE.
